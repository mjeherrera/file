History of Computers

Computers in the 1600s
Year Event
1617 John Napier invented Napier Bones, a manual calculating apparatus also known as Napier Rods.
1642 Biase Pascal invented Pascaline, the world's first automated calculator.
1673 Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz invented the Stepped Reckoner, an improved version of Pascaline.
Computers in the 1800s
Year Event
1801 Joseph Marie Jacquard created a loom using wooden punch cards to automate woven fabric designs.
1822 Charles Babbage invented a steam-driven calculating machine capable of computing tables of numbers.
1822 Charles Babbage invented the Difference Engine for performing a range of calculations.
1840 Lady Ada Lovelace wrote her first computer program, a sequence of instructions for the computer.
1890 Herman Hollerith designed the punch card system to calculate the 1880 U.S. census, saving the government $5 million.
Computers from the 1900s-1950s
Year Event
1936 Alan Turing developed the concept of the Turing machine, laying the foundation for modern computing.
1937 J.V. Atanasoff began building the first computer without cams, belts, or gears.
1939 Hewlett-Packard was founded; Walt Disney used HP 200A Audio Oscillator to test equipment for Fantasia.
1939 Bell Labs completed The Complex Number Calculator designed by George Stibitz.
1941 J.V. Atanasoff and Clifford Berry designed a computer that could solve 29 equations simultaneously.
1941 Konrad Zuse created the Z3 computer, the world's first programmable computer.
1941 Alan Turing and Harold Keen built the British Bombe to decrypt Nazi ENIGMA communications.
1943 ENIAC, the first digital computer, was built by John Mauchly and J. Presper Eckert.
1944 Electronic Discrete Variable Automatic Computer (EDVAC) built, an early electronic computer.
1951 UNIVAC, the first commercially produced digital computer, was launched.
1944 Colossus, designed by Tommy Flowers to break Nazi codes, was introduced.
1945 John von Neumann wrote The First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC, outlining stored-program computer architecture.
1946 UNIVAC, the first commercial computer, was built by Mauchly and Eckert.
1947 Transistor invented by Walter Brattain, William Shockley, and John Bardeen at Bell Laboratories.
1947 Kathleen Booth invented assembly language, bridging software with hardware.
1948 Small-Scale Experimental Machine developed at the University of Manchester, the first high-speed electronic random access memory.
1950 The Standards Eastern Automatic Computer (SEAC) was built, the first stored-program computer in the U.S.
1953 Grace Hopper developed COBOL, the first computer language allowing English-like instructions.
1954 FORTRAN, a major programming language, was developed by John Backus and IBM.
1958 Jack Kirby and Robert Noyce invented the integrated circuit, now known as the computer chip.
Computers from the 1980s-1990s
Year Event
1981 IBM releases the Acorn personal computer with an Intel chip and color monitor.
1982 Time Magazine names the computer its "Machine of the Year."
1983 The CD-ROM is introduced, holding 550 megabytes of data; Microsoft introduced Word.
1984 Apple launches Macintosh with a graphical user interface and mouse.
1985 Microsoft announces Windows, supporting multitasking with a graphical interface.
1986 Pixar is created and later purchased by Steve Jobs, becoming a major player in computer animation.
1990 Tim Berners-Lee develops HTML and prototypes the WorldWideWeb.
1991 Apple releases the Powerbook series of laptops.
1993 Apple releases Newton, the first "Personal Data Assistant"; Jurassic Park showcases advanced computer animation.
1995 IBM releases the ThinkPad 701C; Windows 95 is launched with a major promotional campaign; Java 1.0 and JavaScript introduced.
1996 Sergey Brin and Larry Page develop Google; Palm Pilot is created; Sony Vaio series introduced.
1997 Microsoft invests $150 million in Apple, ending legal disputes over operating system designs.
1998 Apple releases the iMac; the first quantum computer (2-qubit) is launched.
1999 The term Wi-Fi becomes popular; Apple releases the Airport Wi-Fi router.
Computers from 2000-2010
Year Event
2000 SoftBank introduces the first camera phone in Japan; the USB flash drive is introduced.
2001 Apple introduces Mac OS X; Microsoft unveils Windows XP; Apple opens its first stores and releases iTunes.
2003 Apple releases iTunes Music Store; Blu-ray optical disc is introduced; Myspace is founded.
2004 Mozilla releases Firefox 1.0; Facebook launches.
2005 YouTube is founded; Google acquires Android.
2006 Apple unveils the MacBook Pro; the One Laptop per Child program is announced; Amazon Web Services is launched.
2007 Apple releases the iPhone; Amazon releases the Kindle; Dropbox is founded.
2008 Apple releases the MacBook Air.
2009 Microsoft launches Windows 7.
2010 Apple releases the iPad, entering the tablet market.
Computers from 2011-present day
Year Event
2011 Google releases the Chromebook; Nest Learning Thermostat is introduced; Steve Jobs passes away; iPhone 4S features Siri.
2012 Facebook hits 1 billion users; Facebook acquires Instagram; Raspberry Pi is released.
2014 The University of Michigan introduces the Micro Mote (M3), the smallest computer in the world; Apple Pay is introduced.
2015 Apple releases the Apple Watch; Microsoft announces Windows 10.
2016 The first reprogrammable quantum computer is created.
2019 Apple announces iPadOS to better support the iPad as it evolves into a computer-like device.
2022 Frontier becomes the first exascale supercomputer, advancing scientific research.
2023 Microsoft releases ChatGPT-powered Bing; OpenAI launches GPT-4; AI PC is introduced with Intel Core Ultra.
